Impact of Taking Ownership of Sales at SSC

In the chart above, the vertical line marks November 2021, when I assumed full ownership of sales operations at SkidSteerCabs. Before this point, I supported marketing and assisted with sales part time. As I transitioned into full responsibility, performance ramped up and then held steady moving forward.

  • Nov 2021: Took full ownership of sales operations.
  • 2022: Revenue grew +67% YoY.
  • 2023: Revenue stabilized at ~+42% vs 2021, while order volume continued to rise, reflecting sustained demand and process gains.
  • Trend: Average monthly revenue rose sharply after November 2021 and remained above pre-start levels.

Other Contributions to Sales Growth

Established Sales Processes

When I took ownership of sales at SSC there was no formal sales process...no follow ups, no pipeline, and little automation. I quickly set up a pipeline in Highlevel CRM, and set up an effective but simple sales process to fully engage with and close leads. I also helped set up our automated follow up system, from abandoned checkout emails, texts to cold leads, and checkins with customers who had already purchased.

Set Up New Partners

I spearheaded setting up new partnerships with numerous suppliers, including attachment manufacturers Eterra, Digga, and Haugen, along with the Heavy Equipment parts suppliers AMS Parts and Depco. This required a large website effort to add and optimize the new products, learning about the mechanical specifications for heavy equipment attachments, and selling the new products to customers. I tackled the challenge, and those various distributors now form a major part of SSCs business.
